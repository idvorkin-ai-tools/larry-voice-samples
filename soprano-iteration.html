Soprano iteration — can Gemini critique its own voice?

Experiment (2026-04-16): close a generator/critic feedback loop entirely inside Google's Gemini family. Generator: gemini-3.1-flash-tts-preview (Charon base voice + Soprano style directive). Critic: gemini-3.1-pro-preview — which, unlike Claude Opus 4.6, accepts audio input natively. Loop: render WAV → feed back to critic → critic returns score + 3 concrete prompt modifications → append → regenerate. 3 iterations.

Test phrase

"Igor. Listen to me here. You're thinkin' too much. Just go talk to her already."

Score trajectory

IterationScoreCritic's one-line verdict
Iter 1 (baseline) 3/10 "This sounds like a whiny, cartoonish mob henchman rather than the physically imposing, deeply resonant, and heavy-breathing Tony Soprano."
Iter 2 (after 3 mods) 3/10 "The voice is far too light, nasal, and rushed, sounding more like a generic mob caricature than the physically imposing, deep-chested Tony Soprano."
Iter 3 (after 6 mods) 4/10 "The exaggerated breathing and pleading upward inflection on the final line turn Tony into a cartoonish caricature rather than a quiet, menacing boss."

Takeaway: naive append-based iteration produced one point of improvement over three rounds. The critic's iter 3 verdict explicitly called out over-correction from iter 1's "insert heavy breathing" modification — the modification stuck in the prompt and iter 3 produced cartoonishly loud breathing, which the critic then penalised. A production loop would need a reconcile/replace step, not pure append.

Still — the critic is coherent. It catches specific acoustic features (nasal whine, vocal fry, downward vs upward pitch inflection, cadence), articulates them in sound-engineering vocabulary, and proposes imperatives that can be dropped straight into a style directive. This is the thing Claude Opus 4.6 still cannot do at all.

Observations

  • Gemini 3.1 Pro is a competent voice critic. Its critiques are acoustically specific ("elongate the vowel in 'talk'", "downward pitch drop at the end to sound dismissive") rather than vibes.
  • Gemini 3.1 Flash TTS is a competent but conservative generator. It applies pitch/rate/cadence instructions audibly, but doesn't fully transform timbre. Charon stays recognizable as Charon across all 3 iterations.
  • Pure append is the wrong fusion strategy. By iter 3 the prompt contained both "insert audible heavy nasal breathing" (iter 1) and — by iter 3's critique — needed "reduce breathing to subtle ambient nose-breaths". The generator obeyed iter 1, and iter 3's critic penalised the result. A real loop needs diff-then-reconcile: identify which prior mod caused the regression and replace it rather than layering on top.
  • Ceiling ≈ 4–5/10 without voice cloning. The critic's iter-3 verdict implicitly acknowledges that Charon's native timbre isn't Gandolfini's. To crack 7/10 we'd need either (a) speaker-cloning (restricted / risky) or (b) a base voice with more chest resonance than Charon.

Cost & rate-limit notes

Each critic call used ≈ 100 input tokens (audio-modality) plus a few hundred output tokens. At Gemini 3.1 Pro Preview's published audio-input pricing of $2.00 / 1M tokens (≤ 200k prompt tier), three iterations cost << $0.01 in critic spend. Generator spend (3 × ~4-8 s of audio) was roughly $0.05–0.10 at Flash TTS rates. Critic was far cheaper than the generator — inverting the usual "inference is cheap, analysis is expensive" intuition.

No parallelism used; calls were serialized to stay well under any preview-tier rpm cap.

v2 — Tournament-style loop with dimensional scorecard

Experiment v2 (2026-04-16): v1 plateaued at 4/10. The redesign addresses five specific defects: (1) base-voice sweep before prompt tuning instead of locking on Charon; (2) dimensional scorecard (six dimensions, 0-10 each) instead of a single overall score; (3) structured prompt with six named slots and slot-targeted replacement instead of monotonic append; (4) tournament-style round with three candidates ranked by the critic; (5) regression guard that reverts a slot change if any strength dimension drops by more than 1 from the Enceladus baseline. Budget: ~20 TTS + ~20 critic calls, early-stop on two flat rounds.

Final prompt (structured — six slots)

Speak from deep in the chest with a heavy bass-baritone resonance, as if the voice is anchored in the sternum rather than the head. Introduce heavy sinus congestion and audible, labored nasal breathing between phrases to simulate years of heavy smoking and physical weight. Short sentences with a shrugged, unhurried cadence. Pauses are deliberate beats, not hesitations. Slightly lethargic, never rushed. Anchor the pitch in a low, authoritative baritone register, eliminating any upward lilt to maintain a flat, quietly menacing presence. Blend subtle, sustained vocal fry into the mid-vowels, not isolated rattles at sentence endings. Channel James Gandolfini as Tony Soprano in an intimate indoor scene — North-Jersey Italian-American, affectionate default, quiet menace underneath. Drop the final '-g' in gerunds (thinkin', doin'). Never shout.

Delta from v1 baseline: (a) Charon → Enceladus, (b) NASAL_QUALITY slot now names "heavy sinus congestion + labored nasal breathing" explicitly, (c) PITCH slot says "anchor in a low baritone register, flat + quietly menacing" (replaced the v1-era "downward inflection authority" slot that was producing pleading intonation).

Observations vs v1

  • Base-voice choice dominates prompt tuning. Switching from Charon to Enceladus delivered +2 overall on its own (3→5). The best v1 Charon-on-tuned-prompt (4/10) lost to raw Enceladus-on-seed-prompt (5/10). The lesson: optimize the substrate before optimizing the directive.
  • The regression guard works. R3/C1 hit overall=6 by gaining nasal/fry at the cost of pitch_authority (6→4). In v1's naive loop that candidate would have been adopted and the character's "quiet menace" authority would have collapsed. The strict-anchor check against Enceladus baseline caught it.
  • The critic is acoustically specific and consistent. Across all 14 critic calls in v2, the "next_prompt_mods" always named a valid slot and produced replacement directives in clinical acoustic language ("sub-laryngeal resonance", "reduced spectral tilt", "forward formant placement"). Temperature 0.3 (down from v1's 0.4) helped cross-round score consistency.
  • Single-slot mutation revealed a coupling bug. R2/C1 (CHEST_RESONANCE mutation) scored chest=7 — highest chest of any run in either generation — but pacing collapsed to 4. Directives that push one dimension hard can cause the generator to over-rotate prosody elsewhere. Tournament ranking by overall correctly routed around this; monotonic-append would have layered the chest directive and paid the pacing cost anyway.
  • Ceiling: 6/10 without speaker cloning, probably 7/10 with two more rounds. R3/C1's overall=6-with-pitch-regression suggests a "pareto frontier" at around chest=5 pitch=6 pacing=8 — but cracking 8/10+ likely requires either (a) a base voice with even more sub-bass than Enceladus, (b) multi-slot tournaments (mutate 2 slots per candidate to escape coupling traps), or (c) speaker-cloning on a Gandolfini reference — which was flagged out-of-scope for safety and licensing.
